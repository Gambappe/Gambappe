Receipts × XTrace

The app already stamps every pick with proof. XTrace lets us stamp the reason — and revise it in public when reality proves us wrong.

10 concepts Tier 1 flagship → Tier 3 wild honors the ticket system P1–P12 checked zero credentials collected

00 · Why this pairing is uncanny

The fingerprint was always a belief system. We just never let anyone read it.

Receipts already rebuilds a per-user fingerprint nightly — chalk affinity, contrarianism, category profile — a private vector that decides your nemesis, your house, your rating. It's a black box that quietly changes its mind about you every night and never says why.

XTrace is a belief-revision engine: it stores structured beliefs, each with provenance — where it came from, when, and why — and when reality shifts it adds, supersedes, or retracts a belief while preserving the whole history. That is not an adjacent product. That is the fingerprint engine with the lights turned on.

Receipts already has

Timestamped, price-stamped picks. A nightly fingerprint. Public records. "The loser is the protagonist." Receipts over claims.

≅

XTrace already is

Beliefs with provenance edges. Add / supersede / retract with preserved history. Encrypted so even the host can't read it. Session briefings that kill cold-start.

The one-sentence pitch

Today a receipt proves what you picked and when. Put the engine on XTrace and the receipt also carries why — a belief with provenance you can audit, that dies on the record when it betrays you, that you own and can carry anywhere, and that the app itself can't read the private half of.

Concepts are ranked strongest → wildest. Each is scored against the repo's own lateral-development checklist (P1–P12 + hard lines) at the end. Every mockup below is a proposal; nothing here ships as drawn.

01

The Glass Engine

Rebuild the fingerprint as an XTrace belief-log. Every trait becomes a belief with provenance — the black box turns to glass, and the self-model becomes as auditable as a pick.

Why a judge applauds

Every prediction startup demos a black-box "profile." We demo one you can audit: tap any trait and see the exact picks that formed it, the belief it replaced, and the night it flipped. That's Principle 4 — receipts over claims — applied to the app's own opinion of you.

It's not a bolt-on. The nightly fingerprint job already computes these vectors; XTrace just stores each axis as a belief with Derived_From / Supersedes edges instead of overwriting a row. The rating, nemesis matching, and house sort all read the same log — one engine, now legible.

02

The Why-Stamp

At pick time, seal the belief that drove the pick into encrypted memory. On settle, the receipt narrates the belief that won — or names the one that got you killed — and the engine revises it on the spot.

Why a judge applauds

The brand is losing publicly with style. Right now the busted card says you were wrong. With the why-stamp it says what belief betrayed you — and shows the engine downgrading that belief in real time. Loss content that also teaches the model: the obituary becomes a training signal, live on the share card.

Because the "why" is sealed at pick time, it can't be edited after the fact to look smart. It's a receipt on your reasoning, not just your position — the honesty the whole product is built on, extended one layer deeper.

Why a judge applauds

A prediction game lives or dies on "did they really lock that before the market moved?" Today the answer is we promise. XTrace's AES-256 client-side seal (plus homomorphic vector search on the ciphertext) turns the sealed pick into something a skeptical stranger can verify without trusting us — the literal test of Principle 4.

It also makes the reveal a genuine cryptographic moment: two ciphertext blocks stare each other down until the lock, then decrypt in sync. That's Principle 8 — a real appointment — with a mechanism, not just a countdown.

Why a judge applauds

The creator wedge in the PRD is "a link that proves your record." XTrace makes it a link that proves your record and that you own it — the belief-log travels across Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, or the next app, because it was never trapped in ours. Anti-lock-in is XTrace's whole thesis; here it becomes a user-facing promise.

And it sharpens the privacy story the PRD already tells: public means public, but the sensitive half (the raw "why," draft beliefs mid-revision) is encrypted such that the host can't read it. Best-of-both, provable.

05

The Morning Briefing

XTrace's session briefing — active beliefs, current artifacts, open threads — becomes the daily drop's cold open. "Here's where you left off, and here's what the engine changed its mind about overnight."

Why a judge applauds

Cold-start is the enemy of a daily-appointment app. XTrace's briefing pattern was built to eliminate exactly that — you return and the agent opens with where you left off. Re-skinned as a departures-board, it makes the 9:00 knock feel like the app remembered your open stakes, not just fired a generic push.

Crucially it works for a user with zero friends (P5): your sweats, your rival, your own shifting self-model are the content. The overnight belief-revision line — "the engine changed its mind about you" — is a hook nothing else in the category has.

Why a judge applauds

Principle 12 says matchmaking maximizes style contrast, never skill gap — every match must feel winnable. A shadow lets a nervous user rehearse against their rival's actual style before Monday, defusing the blowout fear that kills competitive modes. The app supplies the sparring partner; you don't need a friend (P1, P5).

It only ever reads the public log — no persona of a private self, no account access. The persona is a mirror the engine builds, and it feeds realized results back as a matchmaking signal.

Secret-Ballot Houses

Houses vote on the big-event call while encrypted. Paillier is additively homomorphic — so the tally is computed on the ciphertext. The house call is minted without anyone, the app included, seeing a single individual vote.

Why a judge applauds

"The internal argument is content" — but a public individual vote chills honest calls. A secret ballot that still produces a verifiable house tally is a genuinely novel governance primitive, and Paillier's additive homomorphism makes it real, not hand-wavy: the sum of encrypted votes decrypts to the total without ever exposing a voter.

It's Tier 3 because Houses is itself a stretch feature — but as a demo of "provably fair crowd consensus with private ballots," it's the kind of thing that makes a judge sit up.

Why a judge applauds

Principle 6 wants every feature to read from and write to the engine. This one bets on the engine — the fingerprint's nightly revision is the settlement source, so it's ground truth we don't invent (P4). It also stays clean on the hard lines: the stake is points and pride, never a dollar (P10).

It's reflexive and a little vertiginous — you can try to defend a belief by picking in-character all week — which is exactly why it belongs behind a flag until we see whether it deepens the loop or bends it.

The Persona Duo

When your duo partner is offline, their XTrace persona stands in so the deck never goes cold — clearly labeled, points held out of the ladder. A stopgap that keeps chemistry alive between sessions.

Why a judge applauds (and where it's risky)

Duo queue's whole point is turning strangers into friends through repeated real play — so a bot partner cuts against authenticity, and we flag that honestly. The saving grace: it never scores toward the ladder, it's labeled as a persona, and the real partner's picks always override. It keeps a lonely deck warm without faking a rating.

Included as the deliberate edge case: it shows where the persona idea stops being on-thesis, which is as useful to a review as the wins.

B · What actually gets built

SDK map & repo delta

XTrace ships two modules. The mapping onto Receipts is almost one-to-one:

  • x-memEncrypted agent memory → the belief store. Each fingerprint axis is a belief; the nightly job emits add / supersede / retract operations instead of overwriting a vector row. Powers 01, 02, 05, 07, 09.
  • x-vecEncrypted vector search (AES-256 client-side + Paillier) → sealed picks and the homomorphic tally. Powers 03, 08. Style-distance nemesis matching can query encrypted embeddings directly.
  • provenance edgesThe six typed edges (Derived_From, Supersedes, Depends_On, Referenced, Contains, Created_From) render as the belief-card connectors and the "cause of death" stub.
  • session briefingThe built-in briefing object → the Morning Briefing board (05). Maps active beliefs → your sweat, open threads → your rivals, revisions → "the engine changed its mind."

Repo delta. One new BeliefReceipt card in packages/ui (composes the existing TicketFrame — no new perforation/stub CSS, per the §2 grep gate). A provenance adapter in the fingerprint job so it writes belief ops. New surface tiles on /you (Glass Engine, obituaries) and /rivals (shadow spar, sealed reveal). Feature flags xtrace_beliefs, sealed_cipher. No palette additions beyond the belief-violet signature; no engine rewrite — the fingerprint math is unchanged, only its storage becomes a legible, provenance-tracked log.

The 48-hour demo, if you only build one

Ship 01 Glass Engine + 02 Why-Stamp together. One flow: place a pick, seal the "why," watch it settle, and see the engine supersede the belief on the receipt — proof, reason, and revision in a single loop. That's the whole thesis on stage in ninety seconds, and it's the pair a judge remembers.
